Decoding my journey

Everything started during the first lockdown in Spain: Our company announced that they would be sacking quite a few people and I sort of panicked at the prospect of losing my job, but at the same time I saw it as a blessing in disguise: to stop the endless and boring loop of the office rat race.

I immediately started to find something useful and productive to do within the company, something that would justify my existence there. And there it was, the revamp of an internal website that was made with a 20 year old CMS so I had to learn HTML & CSS. It paid off as soon after, I kept on learning on my own and finally decided to do a Bootcamp course at Le Wagon.

I never thought that coding would end up being my career choice, but it shows that life is full of unexpected surprises. Here you will find the last 2 projects lovingly developed during the course.

Paritize

Paritize comes from the word “parity” and aims to bridge the gap in gender diversity in STEM focused businesses. Companies can register to see how well they are doing in terms of female leadership positions. It also offers a range of resources to improve their transparency and increase gender equality in the workplace.

Applied skills: Ruby on Rails, Devise, PG Search, Cloudinary, Simple Form, Bootstrap & SCSS.
